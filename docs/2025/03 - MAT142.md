**Course Description:**
This course provides a comprehensive review of algebra and trigonometry, emphasizing the numerical, symbolic, and graphical representation of functions necessary for Calculus. The curriculum focuses on exploring the properties of polynomial, rational, trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions, including an introduction to limits and continuity.

---

## Key Topics & Concepts

This course establishes the mathematical foundation for advanced Computer Science coursework:

* **Functions (Numerical, Symbolic, Graphical):**
    * **Core Properties:** Solving problems and representing solutions for various nonlinear functions.
    * **Polynomial and Rational Functions:** Analysis of their behavior, intercepts, and asymptotes.
* **Exponential & Logarithmic Functions:**
    * **Modeling:** Solving problems related to exponential growth and decay using logarithmic and exponential equations.
* **Trigonometry:**
    * **Trigonometric Models:** Introduction to trigonometric functions and their application in modeling periodic real-world phenomena.
    * **Unit Circle and Identities:** Essential concepts for understanding wave functions and computational geometry.
* **Calculus Foundations (Limits):**
    * **Introduction to Limits:** Study of limits and continuity, which serve as the conceptual basis for differential calculus (MAT-225).
* **Problem-Solving Strategies:** Application of various problem-solving strategies to real-world scenarios.
