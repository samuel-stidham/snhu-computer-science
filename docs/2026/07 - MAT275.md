**Course Description:**
This course deepens the study of single-variable calculus through advanced integration techniques and the study of infinite series. Students explore how to represent functions as polynomials with infinitely many terms and use parametric curves and polar coordinates to describe the motion of physical objects.

---

## Key Topics & Concepts

This course focuses on extending integration skills and introducing the mathematical framework for infinite summations:

* **Advanced Integration:** Mastering new techniques including substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions, and the use of integration tables.
* **Infinite Series:** Extending addition to infinitely many numbers to represent differentiable functions (exponential, trigonometric, and logarithmic) as power series.
* **Convergence Testing:** Determining if a series converges using various methods:
  * Integral Test
  * Comparison Test
  * Ratio and Root Tests
  * Alternating Series and Absolute Convergence Tests
* **Taylor & Maclaurin Series:** Generating series from infinitely differentiable functions and determining their radius of convergence.
* **Parametric Curves:** Parameterizing curves to determine slopes, lengths, and areas associated with moving objects.
* **Polar Coordinates:** Graphing equations and calculating area and arc length in polar coordinate systems.

## Major Projects & Assignments

The following projects are completed in a simulated internship at "Taylor-Maclaurin Solutions" to assess mathematical and problem-solving capabilities.

1. **Project One: Internship Skills Inventory Round One**
    * **Focus:** Advanced integration techniques and convergence tests for sequences and series.
    * **Application:** Solving 10 inventory questions covering expectation, mass of a lamina, area, arc length, and specific series tests (Integral, Comparison, and Root tests).

2. **Project Two: Internship Skills Inventory Round Two**
    * **Focus:** Power series, Taylor series, and non-Cartesian coordinate systems.
    * **Application:** Completing 8 assessment questions focused on determining the radius of convergence, generating Maclaurin series, binomial approximations, and analyzing parametric and polar curves (including cycloid area and length).

3. **Collaborative Problem Solving**
    * **Focus:** Peer-to-peer technical assistance on weekly problem sets.
    * **Activity:** Engaging in non-mandatory discussions to troubleshoot complex calculus problems without sharing direct answers.
